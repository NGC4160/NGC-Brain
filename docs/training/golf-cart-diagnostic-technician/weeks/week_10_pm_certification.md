### Key Teaching Points
- PM prevents the expensive roadside/fleet-down failures taught in Weeks 2–7.
- Lithium fleets still need inspection PM (connections, charger, software/BMS health) — different tasks, not “zero maintenance.”
- Certificates document competence — employers care about safety + process.

### Common Misconceptions
| Misconception | Correction |
|---|---|
| “PM is just washing carts.” | Structured inspections, torques, tests, records. |
| “Annual PM is enough for rental fleets.” | Daily/weekly checks catch unsafe carts early. |
| “If it runs, skip documentation.” | Fleets live/die by records and repeatability. |

### Safety Reminders
- Final practical still full PPE/LOTO rules — assessment conditions are real.
- No sharing answers on written exam.
- Road tests during practical only with clearance.

## Week 10 Quiz (7 questions) — Optional mini-check before final; not a substitute for 50-question exam

1. Daily pre-use checks commonly include:  
   A) Full armature rebuild  
   B) Tires, obvious damage/leaks, functional brakes, unusual noises, basic battery condition cues  
   C) Controller reflash only  
   D) Transaxle overhaul  

2. Annual/hourly major PM may include:  
   A) Ignoring batteries  
   B) Full inspection, battery bank load testing, controller diagnostics, transaxle service as scheduled  
   C) Removing all fuses permanently  
   D) Disabling HPD  

3. True or False: Lithium carts require no inspections because they need no watering.

4. A case study presentation should include:  
   A) Symptom → process → data → diagnosis → repair → verification  
   B) Only the part invoice  
   C) Customer passwords  
   D) Unrelated stories only  

5. Short answer: Name two OEM technician training brands referenced in this program.  
6. Short answer: What attendance guideline supports certificate eligibility in the Program Guide?  
7. Short answer: Why keep PM records in a fleet operation?

### Answer Key

1. **B**  
2. **B**  
3. **False** — still inspect connections, charger, BMS/health, mechanical systems  
4. **A**  
5. Club Car, EZ-GO (Textron), Yamaha (any two)  
6. ≥85% attendance or completed makeup labs (per Program Guide)  
7. Accountability, warranty/defense, trend spotting, scheduling, safety compliance
