## Instructor Preparation Guide (Complete Day Before)

### Pre-session setup
- [ ] Select carts with diverse mechanical wear if possible (brakes, play in steering, tire wear)
- [ ] Jack stands rated for cart; wheel chocks; never rely on jack alone
- [ ] Brake measurement tools; brake clean (low-VOC as policy); catch pans
- [ ] Gear oil for transaxle check; correct fluid type card
- [ ] Print `labs/W08_mechanical_inspection.md` and road-test sheet section
- [ ] Mark safe road-test loop; radios/spotter rules
- [ ] Note regen vs mechanical brake interaction for electric carts

### Conditions to highlight (not all must be planted)
| Condition | Teaching value |
|---|---|
| Low tire pressure one side | Pull / range loss |
| Parking brake out of adjustment | Safety fail |
| Wet/contaminated brakes | Noise / poor braking |
| Torn CV/axle boot or seepage (if equipped/visible) | Leak awareness |
| Excess steering play | Kingpin/tie rod inspection |

### Safety
- Hydraulic brake fluid: paint/eye hazard; use correct fluid only.
- Lift points per OEM; jack stands before going under.
- Road tests: helmets if shop policy; max training speed; instructor approval.

### Key Teaching Points
- Regen reduces mechanical brake wear but does **not** replace a working friction brake system.
- Tire pressure is a “range complaint” root cause often missed.
- Noise diagnosis: reproduce safely, localize front/rear/left/right, load vs coast.
- Parking brake must hold on shop’s designated grade test.

### Common Misconceptions
| Misconception | Correction |
|---|---|
| “Electric carts don’t need brake maintenance.” | Mechanical brakes still critical for stops/parking. |
| “All pulls are alignment.” | Brakes, tires, and bent components also pull. |
| “Topping off gear oil fixes bearing noise.” | Find leak source; noise may mean imminent failure. |

### Safety Reminders
- Never work under cart supported only by jack.
- Control dust; avoid compressed-air blasting of brake dust toward face.
- After brake work: pump/adjust per system; test at low speed first.

## Week 8 Quiz (8 questions)

1. Regenerative braking:  
   A) Eliminates need for parking brakes  
   B) Converts some kinetic energy electrically and works with mechanical brakes  
   C) Waters batteries  
   D) Aligns steering  

2. A cart that pulls left under braking may have:  
   A) Only a software update need  
   B) Contaminated/misadjusted brakes, tire issues, or mechanical defects — needs systematic inspection  
   C) Too much windshield fluid  
   D) A good alignment by definition  

3. Before going under a raised cart:  
   A) Trust the jack alone  
   B) Use rated jack stands on proper lift points + chocks  
   C) Have a coworker sit in the seat  
   D) Remove all stands for access  

4. Tire pressure affects:  
   A) Only cosmetics  
   B) Handling, tire life, and effective range  
   C) Controller flash color  
   D) Spark plug gap  

5. True or False: Parking brake performance should be verified as part of mechanical inspection.

### Short Answer

6. List three items checked during a steering play inspection.  
7. What gear oil observations would concern you?  
8. Why use a driver + observer during training road tests?

### Answer Key

1. **B**  
2. **B**  
3. **B**  
4. **B**  
5. **True**  
6. Tie rod ends, kingpins/bushings, rack mounts, wheel bearings, etc.  
7. Low level, burnt smell, metal glitter, milky (water), active leaks at seals  
8. One focuses on vehicle control/safety; one records noises, pulls, conditions, speeds
