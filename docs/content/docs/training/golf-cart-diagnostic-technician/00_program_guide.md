# Program Guide — Golf Cart Diagnostic Technician Training Program

**Version:** 1.1 · July 2026 · Confidential Training Material  
**Contact hours:** 40 · **Sessions:** 20 · **Recommended hands-on ratio:** ≥60%

---

## 1. Purpose & Graduate Outcomes

This program equips technicians to accurately diagnose modern golf carts — electric (flooded lead-acid and lithium) and gas-powered — using safety-first habits and a systematic 7-step diagnostic method.

**Upon successful completion, graduates will be able to:**

1. Apply LOTO, PPE selection, and high-current / chemical hazard controls on golf cart systems.
2. Use DMM, load tester, hydrometer/refractometer, and basic diagnostic tools correctly and safely.
3. Diagnose battery, charger, motor, controller, throttle, solenoid, and wiring faults on Club Car, EZ-GO, Yamaha, and common aftermarket systems.
4. Read OEM wiring diagrams and apply the 7-step diagnostic methodology to real symptoms.
5. Inspect brakes, suspension, steering, and drivetrain for common failure modes.
6. Differentiate lead-acid vs lithium diagnostics and explain BMS protective functions.
7. Execute preventive maintenance and document findings to professional shop standards.

## 3. Assessment System & Rubrics

### 3.1 Grading Weights (suggested)

| Component | Weight | Passing |
|---|---:|---|
| Weekly quizzes (average) | 15% | ≥70% each or average ≥70% |
| Lab worksheets / competency sign-offs | 30% | All critical safety sign-offs = Pass |
| Week 7 & case study documentation | 15% | Rubric ≥3.0 / 4.0 |
| Final practical skills assessment | 25% | Rubric ≥3.0 / 4.0 on all critical items |
| Written final exam (50 questions) | 15% | ≥70% (35/50) |

**Certificate requirement:** Pass all critical safety competencies; final practical ≥3.0; written ≥70%; attendance ≥85% (or makeup labs completed).

### 3.2 Performance Scale (used on practical rubrics)

| Score | Label | Descriptor |
|---:|---|---|
| 4 | Proficient | Correct, safe, complete with minimal cues; professional documentation |
| 3 | Competent | Correct outcome; minor omissions or one non-critical cue |
| 2 | Developing | Incomplete process; multiple cues; safety prompt required once |
| 1 | Unsatisfactory | Incorrect diagnosis, unsafe practice, or critical step skipped |
| 0 | Not attempted / Unsafe stop | Instructor stops work for safety |

**Critical items (must score ≥3):** PPE, LOTO/zero-energy verify, meter category/probe discipline, no jewelry, no interlock bypass.

### 3.3 Documentation Rubric (reports & case studies)

| Criterion | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Symptom verification | Clear, specific, verified | Mostly clear | Vague | Missing |
| Test data | Complete, units, conditions noted | Minor gaps | Incomplete | None / invented |
| Reasoning chain | Logical isolation path | Mostly logical | Jumps to parts | Parts-swap mentality |
| Recommendation | Actionable & justified | Adequate | Weak | Incorrect |
| Safety notes | Explicit | Present | Implied only | Ignored |

## 5. Program Format & Weekly Rhythm

- **Session 1 (Theory/Demo, 2 hrs):** Concepts, demos, whiteboard/schematics, short knowledge checks.
- **Session 2 (Lab, 2 hrs):** Stations, planted faults, worksheets, debrief.
- **Every session:** Opening safety (2–5 min), closing cleanup & parking (5–10 min).
- **Homework (optional, 15–30 min):** Read handout for next week; complete short reflection on lab data.

## 6. Prerequisites & Target Audience

**Prerequisites:** Basic mechanical aptitude; ability to follow written procedures; valid driver’s license for moving carts. Prior small-engine or 12/24/48V experience helpful but not required.

**Audience:** New golf cart techs; maintenance staff at courses/resorts/HOAs/fleets; experienced mechanics specializing; supervisors deepening diagnostic capability.

## 7. Required Equipment & Facilities (summary)

See also weekly instructor prep lists and Appendix-equivalent tool list in Week 1 materials.

**Must have:** 4–6 carts (mixed), CAT III DMMs, battery load tester (100A+), hydrometer/refractometer + temp chart, hand tools, torque wrenches, crimpers, PPE (glasses, Class 00 gloves, leather over-gloves, face shields), Class C/ABC extinguisher, spill kit, eyewash, ventilation for charging, OEM manuals (Club Car, EZ-GO, Yamaha), Curtis fault-code reference (print or https://faultcodes.curtisinstruments.com/).

**Nice to have:** Curtis/Alltrax programmer, megger, training boards, large schematic display, lithium OEM app/tooling.

## 8. Scaling Notes

| Situation | Adaptation |
|---|---|
| **Small class (≤4)** | More one-on-one coaching; each student gets full cart ownership for case study |
| **Large class (10–12)** | Split Session 2 into A/B rotations; second instructor or senior tech as proctor |
| **Fleet-only (one brand)** | Keep multi-brand theory; deepen labs on fleet OEM diagrams |
| **Lithium-heavy shop** | Expand Week 3; add BMS app stations; still teach lead-acid (legacy fleets) |

| **Compressed delivery** | Not recommended below 40 hrs; if forced, merge Week 8 mechanical into Week 7 debrief and Week 10 |
| **Online theory hybrid** | Theory may be remote; **all LOTO, battery, high-current, gas labs must be in-person** |

## 9. Customization Tips

1. **Insert shop SOPs** (torque specs, work-order software screenshots) into Week 7 documentation block.
2. **Plant faults that match your common comebacks** (high-resistance cables, weak solenoids, mis-set HPD).
3. **Localize PM schedules** to your climate (Coastal humidity → corrosion emphasis; cold climates → lithium charge-temp limits).
4. **Career pathways:** Tailor Week 10 to dealer OEM paths vs independent shop vs internal fleet promotion.
5. **Do not** teach mobile trip charges or discontinued product lines as current practice unless historical context is labeled “legacy.”
6. Update Curtis/Alltrax model list annually; always require model-year-correct diagrams.

## 10. The 7-Step Diagnostic Process (Program Spine)

Taught in Week 7; practiced Weeks 2–10; assessed on final practical.

1. **Verify the complaint** — Reproduce or clearly define the symptom.
2. **Gather information & history** — Interview, service records, recent work, environment.
3. **Visual & safety inspection** — PPE, damage, corrosion, leaks, loose hardware.
4. **Isolate systems** — Power → control → propulsion → mechanical; start with batteries/connections.
5. **Test components** — Measure, don’t guess; record values with units.
6. **Perform repair** — Correct root cause; torque/specs; no safety bypasses.
7. **Verify repair & road test** — Confirm fix under load; document.

> **Shop mantra:** Diagnose before replace.

## 11. Attendance, Makeup & Conduct

- Missed theory: complete reading + quiz makeup before next lab.
- Missed lab: scheduled makeup with instructor before Week 10 finals.
- Jewelry, horseplay, or intentional interlock defeat → immediate stop + coaching; repeat → dismissal from session.
