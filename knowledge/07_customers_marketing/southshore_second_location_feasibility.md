# Southshore Second Location — Market Analysis & Feasibility Study

**Prepared for:** Ryan & Christine White, Neighborhood Golf Carts  
**Date:** 2026-07-09  
**Scope:** Second shop on the Southshore of Lake Pontchartrain (Metairie / Jefferson / Kenner), with **free pickup & delivery up to 40 miles** from that shop  
**Home base:** 71363 Thelma Ln, Suite E, Covington, LA 70433  
**Analyst framing:** Golf-cart repair / lithium-conversion specialty shop expansion (not a new-cart dealership)

---

## Executive verdict

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **Is there a real Southshore market?** | **Yes.** Jefferson Parish alone (~427k–431k people) is larger than St. Tammany (~278k). Kenner’s 2024 street-legal golf-cart ordinance and Harahan’s ≤25 mph ordinance are structural demand drivers. |
| **Is a second shop feasible?** | **Conditionally yes** — but **not as the next move**. Covington is still capacity- and process-constrained (~$555k revenue, thin ~$32k net, lithium SLA / WIP risk). Opening Location 2 before Location 1 is predictable will dilute Ryan/Christine and burn cash. |
| **Best site among Metairie / Jefferson / Kenner?** | **Kenner first**, then **Elmwood–Harahan (Jefferson industrial)**, then **Metairie last** (highest competition density). |
| **Recommended path** | **Phase A (now):** Prove Southshore demand from Covington with a paid → free pilot. **Phase B:** Open a lean Southshore satellite only after Covington hits throughput gates. |

**Bottom line:** The Southshore is a legitimate second-market opportunity for NGC’s shop-only + lithium model. Free 40-mile Southshore P&D is the right *offer* for that market — but the right *sequence* is demand proof and Covington stability first, brick-and-mortar second.

### Why Southshore is structurally hard from Covington

- Lake Pontchartrain + Causeway (~24 miles) is a **hard logistics barrier**. Customers will not trailer carts across for routine service; NGC must haul them.
- Current Southshore pricing (~$99 round-trip in pricebook) is a **conversion tax** vs Northshore free P&D — it suppresses volume even when willingness to pay for repair exists.
- Roy is a **single-driver bottleneck**. Scaling Southshore volume from Covington without a second bay/driver plan will crush Causeway utilization and shop WIP.

### Policy conflict to resolve before marketing Location 2

Knowledge and pricebook still treat Southshore as **paid**. A second location with **free 40-mile Southshore P&D** is a **new policy decision** — log it when confirmed, update `shop_services.md` / `market.md`, and deactivate conflicting HCP lines.

### Regulatory demand map (critical for golf-cart repair)

Louisiana RS 32:299.4: carts on public roads only where parish/city designates streets.

| Jurisdiction | Street-legal status | Implication for NGC |
|--------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Kenner** | **Yes** — city streets ≤35 mph (2024 ordinance); $75/yr registration + inspection | Strongest Southshore **usage growth** catalyst; more street miles → more wear, lighting, brakes, batteries, lithium upgrades |
| **Harahan** | **Yes** — streets ≤25 mph (ord. ~2020); Jefferson Hwy restrictions | Dense pocket near Colonial Golf & CC |
| **Metairie / unincorporated Jefferson** | **No broad street ordinance** found | Demand skews to **private roads, HOAs, courses, commercial/utility** — still real, less “neighborhood cruiser” volume than Kenner |
| **Orleans / English Turn** | Gated / private community use | High-ticket residential; fleet-of-one lithium buyers |

**Analyst note:** Kenner’s ordinance is the single best *forward* demand signal on the Southshore. Shops that ignore Kenner will miss the growth node; shops that only chase Metairie retail corridors will fight dealers on their home turf.

### Demand pockets (priority outreach list)

1. **Kenner — Chateau Estates / Chateau Golf & Country Club** — neighborhood + course adjacency + street legality  
2. **Harahan — Colonial Golf & Country Club corridor** — ordinance + private club density  
3. **Metairie Country Club / Old Metairie residential** — wealth, not street-legal volume  
4. **English Turn (East Bank)** — gated golf community; lithium-ready AOV  
5. **Commercial / municipal / industrial** — schools, venues, facilities using Club Car / EZGO fleets (compete with Golf Cars of Louisiana on service responsiveness, not on OEM sales)

| Competitor | Location | Model | Strength | NGC wedge |
|------------|----------|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Golf Cars of Louisiana** | 8057 Airline Dr, Metairie | Club Car **authorized distributor** for LA; sales + certified service + P&D | Brand authority, parts pipeline, multi-location scale | Independent, all-makes diagnostics; **lithium conversion specialist**; faster personal service; not “dealer service department” feel |
| **Deep South Golf Cars** | 2508 Hickory Ave, Metairie | EZGO / Cushman authorized; sales + service + P&D; claims 24–48 hr parts jobs | Local brand, parts depth, custom builds | Same — shop-only craft + Professional Kit lithium + transparent $179 diagnostic |

Both major competitors sit in a **tight Metairie Airline / Hickory cluster** (~1–2 miles apart). That corridor is **saturated for dealership-style sales+service**.

### Indirect / substitute competition

- Mobile “battery guys” and handymen (low trust, no lithium warranty depth)  
- Dealer P&D from Baton Rouge / other GCLA stores (distance disadvantage vs local satellite)  
- DIY lithium Amazon kits (NGC’s 5-year battery+BMS + install quality is the counter)

### Competitive positioning for Location 2

**Do not open as a mini-dealership.** Open as:

> **Southshore’s lithium & diagnostics shop** — all makes, Professional Kits, free local P&D, 2–3 day lithium SLA, $179 diagnostic that applies toward repair.

That is differentiated from Club Car / EZGO sales floors. Cart sales remain **future** per company strategy.

## 5. Free 40-mile Southshore P&D — operating design

### Offer (proposed)

| Zone | Fee |
|------|-----|
| ≤40 miles of **Southshore shop** | **Free** (marketed like Northshore) |
| Outside 40 miles from either shop | Flat fee (keep / revisit $99) |
| Cross-lake (Southshore customer → Covington bay only) | Prefer **route to Southshore shop**; do not advertise free Causeway hauls into Covington once Location 2 is open |

### Capacity math (do not underwrite with hope)

| Assumption | Planning figure |
|------------|----------------:|
| Round-trip local P&D (load, drive, unload) | 45–90 min inside Jefferson core; 2–3 hrs at zone edge |
| Productive driver days | ~8 hrs |
| Sustainable stops/day (batched) | **4–6** round-trips if zoned; **2–3** if random |
| Peak lithium weeks | P&D becomes the constraint before bay labor |

**Implication:** Location 2 needs either (a) a **dedicated Southshore driver** (hire or relocate Roy’s primary beat), or (b) a **shared driver with hard zone days** (e.g., Southshore M/W/F). Free 40-mile without driver capacity is a marketing promise that destroys SLA.

### Toll & fuel

Causeway southbound tolls apply when Covington staff/parts cross. Budget **inter-shop logistics** separately from customer P&D. Prefer stocking lithium kits and common parts **at Location 2** so techs are not Causeway-dependent daily.

## 7. Phased go-to-market (recommended)

### Phase A — Southshore demand proof (from Covington) — do this first

**Goal:** Validate volume and AOV before signing a lease.

1. **Finalize zone map** (already P1 on backlog) — zip list for free Northshore vs paid Southshore.  
2. **90-day Southshore campaign:**  
   - GBP posts + landing copy for Kenner / Metairie / Harahan (shop-only, not mobile).  
   - HOA / Chateau / Colonial outreach.  
   - Offer: diagnostic $179 + **waive or discount P&D** for first N Southshore jobs *or* lithium deposit special — measure close rate.  
3. **Track weekly:** Southshore leads, booked jobs, lithium %, P&D cost per job, Causeway hours.  
4. **Kill criteria:** If after 90 days Southshore cannot sustain **≥8–10 completed jobs/month** at healthy margin even with P&D subsidy, **do not lease**.  
5. **Go criteria:** ≥12–15 Southshore jobs/month **or** ≥4 lithium/month from Southshore with acceptable haul cost → proceed to site search.

### Phase B — Soft satellite (only after Phase A go + Covington gates)

Covington gates before lease:

- In-progress WIP ≤ 6 sustained for 4+ weeks  
- Lithium average days in shop ≤ 3  
- Legacy mobile / NGC Conversion pricebook items deactivated  
- Deposit process reliable (Christine checklist)  
- Driver plan documented for two zones  

Then: Kenner/Elmwood LOI on 2.5–4.5k SF flex; hire or assign **1 tech + shared driver**; mirror Professional Kit SKUs; separate GBP “Neighborhood Golf Carts — Kenner” (or Jefferson).

### Phase C — Full second location

- Second tech when WIP consistently hits cap  
- Dedicated Southshore driver  
- Local parts min/max  
- Revisit cart sales **only after** both shops hit service targets (per existing company strategy)
